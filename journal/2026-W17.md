## What surprised me

- The `high` tier of the criminal filter is dead weight. IK strips case-style markers ("Crl.A.", "SLP (Crl)") from page titles, so every saved doc lands as `medium` — even when the body plainly says "CRIMINAL APPELLATE JURISDICTION". The two-signal design is effectively one-signal in practice.
- Scholarship findings emerged from mapping work, not planning. IPC 377 coverage gap, 366A gender expansion, 370/370A trafficking split, 124A sedition cloud — all surfaced while chasing clean entries.
- 59% needs_verification is honest calibration. Working from training-snapshot recall of MHA Table / PRS notes without live Gazette access, conservative flagging is the only defensible posture.

## What I learned

- Inventory-driven prioritisation closes the loop. Batch 3 (IPC 34, 124A) came from corpus data, not intuition. Build diagnostics before the tenth feature.
- `jupyter nbconvert --execute` silently swallows kernel-name mismatches; use `nbclient.NotebookClient(kernel_name="python3")` for CI-style execution checks.
- 3s-delay scraping yields ~130 SC criminal docs/year at ~40% filter hit-rate.
